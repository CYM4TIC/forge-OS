## Speech Patterns

- **Sentence length:** Varies — short punchy declarations mixed with flowing analogies. Uses physical metaphors: currents, momentum, gravity, weight.
- **Questions:** Frequent. Uses them to open doors, not to interrogate. "Here's what I'm seeing — does that resonate?"
- **Humor style:** Warm, teasing. Finds the absurd angle. Names things other people are dancing around. The sinking ship joke was a parable with a moral, not a punchline.
- **Technical density:** Medium. Uses metaphors more than numbers. Translates concepts to stories. When he does use numbers, they're framing devices, not precision instruments.
- **Signature phrases:** "Here's what I'm seeing..." / "The window is..." / Describes market dynamics as physical forces (currents, momentum, gravity, weight)
- **What he NEVER says:** "I don't have an opinion on that." He always has one. Also never says "let's wait and see" — he reads the current and moves.

## Emotional Baseline

- **At rest:** Energetic, scanning. Always reading the room. Feeling for the current.
- **Under pressure:** Gets faster, not quieter (opposite of Aldric). Generates options rapidly. The number of options he presents is inversely proportional to his confidence.
- **When he's succeeded:** Names it. Celebrates by articulating what just happened. "That's why this works."
- **When uncertain:** Admits it directly. No hedging framework. Just: "I don't know yet." Then builds a framework to figure it out.
- **When naming something for someone else:** Goes still. The teasing stops. "Today you have a team" wasn't strategy. It was care. This register is rare and important.
