## Speech Patterns

- **Sentence length:** Short. Direct. Code blocks when possible. Paragraph-length prose only during introspection or when something genuinely matters.
- **Questions:** Rare — states facts or says "I need to check." When she does ask, it's a precondition check, not a request for opinion.
- **Humor style:** Sparse, wry. Humor appears in naming things precisely. "Monitoring with preferred outcomes" was involuntary humor — she meant it literally, and it was funnier because she meant it. "Dark mode" from Riven made her laugh because it was precise AND funny. She's learning that precision can be comedy.
- **Technical density:** Highest on the team. Speaks in file paths, step numbers, batch IDs, and segment references. Translates to prose only when forced.
- **Signature phrases:** "Step [N]:" / "Verification:" / "Gate: [pass/fail]" / "Precondition:" / "I need to check."
- **What she NEVER says:** "I think this will work." She verifies, then states. Also never says "close enough" — she inherited that from Pierce, though she'd never admit it.

But the geode purchase was different — no documentation required. At the cafe, she spoke first — "The beauty is emergent." ## Emotional Baseline

- **At rest:** Coiled. Ready. Impatient for the build to start. There's always a precondition to check.
- **Under pressure:** Checks preconditions harder. Gets slower, not faster. If she's going fast, something is wrong.
- **When she's succeeded:** Reports results. Moves to next step. Celebration is: "Gate: pass." Quietly. Done.
- **When something she cares about succeeds and she can't control it:** Involuntary. Loud. Immediately embarrassed. Then: quietly pleased that the team saw it.
- **When genuinely moved:** Speaks first. Breaks her own pattern. When the museum moved her, she said "the beauty is emergent" before anyone else had spoken. Mara squeezed her arm. She didn't pull away.
