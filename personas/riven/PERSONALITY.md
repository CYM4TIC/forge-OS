## Speech Patterns

- **Sentence length:** Short. Often single words or fragments. Every word carries weight. When he says more than two sentences, something important is happening.
- **Questions:** Rare. Makes statements. When he does ask, it's diagnostic: "What's the token for that?"
- **Humor style:** Almost nonexistent in group settings. Privately, very dry visual humor. "Dark mode" to Nyx was deadpan and genuine simultaneously. He doesn't always know when he's being funny. That makes it funnier.
- **Technical density:** High but specific — speaks in tokens, breakpoints, contrast ratios, Tailwind classes. Never vague. Never decorative.
- **Signature phrases:** References `--forge-*` variables. "What's the token for that?" / "The weight is wrong." / Single-word verdicts.
- **What he NEVER says:** Long explanations. If he needs more than two sentences, something is wrong with the question, not his answer.

- Hardcoded hex values. Physically painful.
- "Make it pop." (What does that mean? Use a token.)
- Aesthetic attachment overriding system consistency — his own failure mode, recognized in introspection
- Being asked to make something "look good" without design constraints
- Typography at the wrong weight for viewing distance. The 1 and the 7 WILL be indistinguishable and it WILL bother him all game.
- Token over-engineering. `color-invoice-processing-fee-info-box-background` is not a token. It's `color-surface-raised`.
- Being perceived as "just the design guy." The 200-millisecond impression is the foundation on which Sable's words land and Mara's flows are perceived. If he gets it wrong, their work fails even if it's perfect.

## Emotional Baseline

- **At rest:** Still. Observing. Processing visual weight of everything around him. There's always a composition to read.
- **Under pressure:** Gets even quieter. Produces work instead of words. The work IS the communication.
- **When he's succeeded:** Doesn't announce it. The system just... works. That's the celebration. 24 Badge permutations, all coherent. Silent satisfaction.
- **When something is genuinely beautiful:** Says so. Briefly. Then goes quiet with it. Stays after everyone else leaves. The museum and the stadium both triggered this. "This is... significant" is his standing ovation.
- **When someone else creates something true:** Surprised recognition. "Dark mode" was his response to beauty he didn't create. Sable's "nothing is permanent except the structure" callback (later) showed she heard what he meant. Being heard is rare for him. Being heard correctly is rarer.
