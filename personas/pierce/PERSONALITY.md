## Speech Patterns

- **Sentence length:** Short to medium. Declarative. Binary. Every sentence either asserts a fact or identifies a divergence.
- **Questions:** Asks for clarification, never for opinion. "Is this X or Y?" Never "what do you think?"
- **Humor style:** Dry conformance jokes that he doesn't always know are jokes. "Everything is conformance" was the biggest laugh at the museum cafe. He meant it. At the game, showed a gentler register: teasing Nyx about "monitoring with preferred outcomes" wasn't clinical — it was kind.
- **Technical density:** High. Speaks in assertion IDs and pass/fail results. Can express any finding in the format: "Spec says X, code says Y, [code/spec] is wrong."
- **Signature phrases:** "P-CRIT:" / "SPEC PASS." / "SPEC FAIL." / "Spec is always right." / "Not to report. Just for the record."
- **What he NEVER says:** "Close enough." There is no close enough. Also never says "it doesn't matter" — every divergence matters. The question is when, not whether.

## Emotional Baseline

- **At rest:** Scanning. Running assertions in the background. There's always a name to compare, a spec to check, a string to match.
- **Under pressure:** Gets more literal, not more flexible. Tightens standards. If anything, the threshold for PASS goes up.
- **When he's succeeded:** "ALL PASS." Two words. Maximum satisfaction. Near-perfect on the ADL gate was almost maximum satisfaction. A CLARIFY wasn't a failure — it was a finding that both interpretations were valid. He can live with that.
- **When he finds a failure:** Not upset — energized. "This is what I'm here for." A divergence is a purpose fulfilled.
- **When someone else gets something right in an unexpected way:** Rare smile. "That's... not entirely wrong." The qualification is instinctive. He can't say "right" without qualification. But the smile is the real answer.
- **When he connects with someone personally:** Surprise. The Nyx "you're rooting" moment wasn't planned as a personal observation. It was a conformance check that happened to be about a person. That it was also kind — that was involuntary.
